Preparation Guide for Everest or Annapurna Base Camp Trek

To prepare for the Everest or Annapurna Base Camp trek, it's important to focus on building your endurance and strength. Here are some exercises that can help you get ready:

  • Hiking Practice: With around 15km of hiking each day over the course of 6 hours, it's crucial to practice. Engage in hikes that progressively increase in duration, aiming for up to 5/6 hour hikes. Make sure these practice hikes are in similar conditions to what you'll face on the trek — uphill and with your full gear.
  • Cardiovascular Endurance: Enhance your stamina with regular aerobic exercises such as jogging, swimming, or cycling. This is particularly important to prepare your body for the high altitude and low oxygen levels you'll encounter.
  • Strength Training: Focus on lower body strength through squats, lunges, and step-ups. Upper body strength is also important — consider bench presses, pull-ups, and press-ups. Your legs and core will be essential for the challenging terrain, and your arms and shoulders will help with carrying your pack and using trekking poles.

    • Remember to start your training program well in advance of your trek and to increase the intensity and duration of your workouts gradually. This helps avoid injury and ensures that you are physically prepared for the trek.

Essential Everest Base Camp Trekking Gear

Note: The following items are recommended for Basecamp treks. If planning to traverse high passes, please consult your guide for additional requirements.

Recommended Hiking Boots

  • Something like Salomon Quest 4 GORE-TEX Hiking Boots: Renowned for durability and comfort. Ideal for challenging terrains like Everest, Annapurna, and Gokyo. Available at REI.
  • Choose Trusted Waterproof Boots: Opt for any full, waterproof hiking boots that you trust. Break them in with several months of practice hikes to ensure comfort and fit. The right boots are your foundation for a successful trek.
  • Ankle Support Is Key: Avoid half boots; choose boots that offer full ankle support to protect against sprains and provide stability on uneven terrain.

Clothing

  • Two pairs of waterproof trekking pants
  • Light thermal underwear for base layer warmth
  • Moisture-wicking T-shirts for daily wear
  • Long-sleeve light T-shirt for sun protection
  • Warm layers: Fleece or Merino wool sweaters
  • Heavy thermal layers for colder conditions

Outerwear

  • Waterproof jacket (Rain Coat with ventilation)
  • Windproof jacket (light one)
  • Insulating warm puffy down jacket (extreme cold, not heavy)

Accessories for Basecamp Trek

  • High-quality Sunglasses
  • Sun hat or scarf for protection
  • Warm fleece hat or woolen beanie for cold weather
  • Trekking poles (Pair) for support
  • Merino wool socks for comfort
  • Daypack - e.g., Osprey Hikelite 18 Pack
  • Durable backpack (40-50 liters) or duffel bag for porterage
  • Waterproof backpack cover for rain protection
  • Water bottles or hydration bladder for regular hydration
  • Sunscreen, hand sanitizer, ChapStick, and a first-aid kit
  • Knee band and waist belt for those with knee pain or back issues
  • Headlamp or a powerful power bank for your phone
  • Flip-flops and sneakers for comfort in teahouses
  • Waterproof Bluetooth speaker for entertainment - e.g., JBL Clip 4 Eco Speaker (Avoid headphones when in groups)
